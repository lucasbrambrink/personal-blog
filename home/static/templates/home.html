Algorithms

Let's face it: algorithm challenges are fun. Beyond their utility as programmatic excercises, they sharpen our problem solving abilities. It is often said that problem solving is the main ingredient to successful software development.
"We should be solving problems, not building features. Features are attributes, not objectives."

As such, algorithm challenges force us to:

  1. Figure out exactly what components need to be addressed. Knowing this alone is invaluable.
  2. Think about how the ideal solution would behave.
  3. Iterate! Build with purpose, and fail quickly.

"As human beings, we get better at what we practice. No matter what it is. If you practice methodology X, you will get good at it. Therefore, I would like you to ask yourself, where do you think there is more leverage? I don't care what X is. Pick any X you want. Would you rather be good at it or the general skill of solving problems?

- Rich Hickey
